Transport Layer Protocol

The two protocols that operate at the transport layer are TCP and User Datagram Protocol (UDP), as shown in Figure 1. TCP is considered a reliable, full-featured transport layer protocol, which ensures that all of the data arrives at the destination. In contrast, UDP is a very simple transport layer protocol that does not provide for any reliability. IP uses these transport protocols to enable hosts to communicate and transfer data.
