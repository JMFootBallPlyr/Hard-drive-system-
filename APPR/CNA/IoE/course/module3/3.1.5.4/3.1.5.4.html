Connect the Internal Data Cables

Internal drives and optical drives typically connect to the motherboard using SATA data cables.

The SATA data cable has a 7-pin connector, as shown in Figure 1. SATA cables are keyed to connect only one way. Many SATA cables have locking connectors that prevent the cables from coming unplugged. To remove a locked cable, depress the raised metal tab on the plug, and then pull the connector out.

The steps to connect a drive to the motherboard using a SATA data cable include:

Step 1. (Figure 2) Plug one end of the SATA cable into the motherboard socket. Notice that the motherboard has more than one SATA connector.

Step 2. (Figure 3) Plug the other end of the SATA cable into the smaller SATA port on the drive.

Installation Tip: Use the same care when installing SATA data cables as SATA power cables. Also, avoid sharp bends or folds in the cable. These may limit transmission rates.
