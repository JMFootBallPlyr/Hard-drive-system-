LANs

A computer network is identified by the following specific characteristics:

  • Size of the area covered

  • Number of users connected

  • Number and types of services available

  • Area of responsibility

Traditionally, a LAN is defined as a network that encompasses a small geographical area. However, the distinguishing characteristic for LANs today is that they are typically owned by an individual, such as in a home or small business, or wholly managed by an IT department, such as in a school or corporation. This individual or group enforces the security and access control policies of the network.
