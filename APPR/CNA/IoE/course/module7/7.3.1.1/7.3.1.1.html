Modems

IT technicians must understand the purpose and characteristics of common networking devices. This section discusses a variety of devices.

A modem connects to the Internet through an Internet Service Provider (ISP). There are three basic types of modems. Modems convert a computer’s digital data into a format that can be transmitted on the ISP’s network. An analog modem converts digital data to analog signals for transmission over analog phone lines. A digital subscriber line (DSL) modem connects a user’s network directly to the digital infrastructure of the phone company. A cable modem connects the user’s network to a cable service provider, which typically uses a hybrid fiber coax (HFC) network.
